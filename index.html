About Me

Jeremy is a Staff Security Engineer on the Platform Security team at Cruise. For the past three years, he has worked on transforming Cruise's internal Authentication and Authorization layers into a unified global framework spanning hundreds of clusters and thousands of cloud applications. Previously, Jeremy was a Tech Lead at Duo Security, where he's worked on the next generation of passwordless authentication. He also worked on the Duo Labs team, doing exploratory research on zero trust networking and new trust and authentication models. Before that, he did a brief stint as a Ph.D. student at the University of Michigan, where he achieved candidacy but decided the academic life was not for him. His interests primarily fall under the wide category of network and systems security, with a particular focus on Authentication and Authorization technologies. He received his M.S. in Computer Science from the University of California, Davis, with a focus on Android security and privacy. He has previously been a researcher / developer / instructor at Sandia National Laboratories in California where he ran the Center for Cyber Defenders internship program for several years and founded the Cyber Technologies Academy. He also worked on a bunch of other projects there, like FARM and minimega.

Jeremy moved to the Seattle area in 2019 with his wife and now has two kids that keep him busier than ever. When the youngest gets old enough to wear a helmet, he looks forward to family bike rides again.
